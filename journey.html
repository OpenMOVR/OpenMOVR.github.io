Evolution Through Learning

How we built, failed, learned, and evolved over 12 years of registry development

2013-2018: The USNDR Foundation

The Beginning: Launched as the United States Neuromuscular Disease Registry (USNDR) across 4 diseases at 26 sites

What We Built: Manual eCRF system, foundational data collection protocols

Impact: 2,477 enrollments, 9,859 encounters - proving longitudinal tracking was possible

2019-2024: Scaling to MOVR

The Growth: Expanded to 7 diseases, 60+ sites, with 3,408 new enrollments

What We Learned: Manual data entry doesn't scale sustainably

"We entered this field to help people pass away gracefully, but now we have unexpected treatments and are navigating new territory... despite the challenges, we are committed to doing right by these families."
— MOVR Principal Investigator

2025+: Building Next Generation

The Vision: FHIR-based automated EMR integration, open source development

Current Status: Vendor selection and proof-of-concept phase

Voices from Our Network

What our 54 active sites told us during comprehensive interviews (representing 100% participation from all active sites)

💬 What Sites Value Most

  • ✅ Appreciation for MOVR's uniqueness: "Captures data across wide range of neuromuscular diseases"
  • ✅ Patient engagement value: "Patients appreciate the opportunity to participate in research"
  • ✅ Collaborative potential: "Desire for data sharing and benchmarking with other sites"
  • ✅ Research foundation: "Addresses gaps in natural history and real-world treatment outcomes"
"Overall, a desire for MOVR to succeed and make a difference - despite the challenges, many sites expressed desire for MOVR to contribute to improving lives"
— From comprehensive site interviews (38/54 sites)

⚠️ Critical Challenges Identified

  • ⚠️ Data entry burden: "Time-intensive nature of comprehensive data capture"
  • ⚠️ Resource constraints: "Need for adequate staffing and funding"
  • ⚠️ Data quality concerns: "Clarity of outcome measures and representativeness"
  • ⚠️ Limited communication: "Desire for more regular updates and collaboration"
"More is not always better – narrow in on what is relevant and potentially can be helpful"
— Site Principal Investigator feedback

From Problems to Solutions

The specific data challenges we identified and how next-gen technology addresses them

🚨 Challenge: Manual Data Entry

The Problem:
  • 58% of hospitalizations marked as 'other' - unclear safety signals
  • 35-50% missing gross motor milestone data
  • 47% unknown SMN2 copy data in legacy MOVR
  • Data entry burden limiting site participation
Next-Gen Approach:
  • FHIR-based automated EMR extraction
  • Custom ontology for hospitalization classification
  • EMR-derived genetic correlation analysis
  • Reduced coordinator burden through automation

🚨 Challenge: Limited Scope

The Problem:
  • Static eCRF fields limiting adaptability
  • 120+ sites on waiting list due to capacity
  • Single institution health data silos
  • Indication expansion roadblocks
Next-Gen Approach:
  • Scalable multi-health system integration
  • Flexible data models for new indications
  • Cross-institutional data harmonization
  • Open source community development

🚨 Challenge: Industry Demands

The Problem:
  • Regulatory agencies demand more robust real-world evidence
  • Comparative effectiveness requires multi-year, multi-site design
  • Missing patient voice in registry value
  • Limited industry partnership integration
Next-Gen Approach:
  • Enhanced biomarker discovery capabilities
  • Head-to-head treatment comparisons
  • Patient-centered design principles
  • Strategic industry co-development model
