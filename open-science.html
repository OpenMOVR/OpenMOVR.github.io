Collaborative Governance Framework

Transitioning from oversight to co-development with community participation

üèõÔ∏è Evolution of Governance

From RAC Oversight ‚Üí Community Co-Development

  • Previous Model: Research Advisory Committee (RAC) oversight structure
  • New Direction: Collaborative co-development partnerships
  • Participant Voice: Direct input through disease-specific committees
  • Shared Decision-Making: Community involvement in research priorities

MDA's Role & Ethical Framework

While fostering collaboration and community input, MDA maintains ultimate decision-making authority to ensure ethical standards, participant protection, and mission alignment in all research initiatives.

Community Participation Structure

Participant Committees by Disease Indication:

  • ALS Participant Committee: Patient/caregiver research priorities
  • SMA Participant Committee: Community-driven study design input
  • Muscular Dystrophy Committee: Cross-indication collaboration
  • Other Indications: Expanding to all 7 MOVR disease areas

Publication Committees per Disease:

  • Research proposal review and prioritization
  • Publication co-authorship coordination
  • Community benefit assessment
  • Dissemination strategy development

‚öñÔ∏è Ethical Data Stewardship

Core Principles:

  • Participant Benefit: Research must serve community needs
  • Transparency: Open processes and decision documentation
  • Privacy Protection: Rigorous data security and anonymization
  • Equitable Access: Fair distribution of research opportunities
  • Scientific Integrity: Reproducible and ethical research practices

Decision Authority & Accountability:

MDA retains final decision-making authority to ensure:

  • Compliance with ethical standards
  • Participant privacy and safety
  • Mission alignment with neuromuscular disease advocacy
  • Sustainable research practices
