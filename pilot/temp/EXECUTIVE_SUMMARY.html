MOVR 2.0 Pilot Program

Executive Summary

Prepared by: Andre D. Paredes, PhD | Sr. Director of Strategy & Informatics Date: January 2026 Status: Live & Operational

Overview

The MOVR 2.0 Pilot is MDA's initiative to select the next-generation platform for managing medical data for families living with neuromuscular diseases. We are evaluating three vendor platforms through real-world family feedback before committing to a long-term partner.

Target: 500 participants across DMD, SMA, and LGMD Duration: 6 months (participants can opt out anytime) Cost: $0 operational (Microsoft 365 infrastructure)

Key Takeaways

Area Status
Enrollment Live and accepting participants
Automation Fully automated vendor assignment and communications
Vendor Balance System ensures fair distribution across all 3 platforms
Data Security HIPAA-compliant; participant-controlled sharing
Reporting Real-time dashboard via Power BI

Why This Matters

For Families:

  • Modern tools to manage complex medical journeys
  • Visualize and share health data on their terms
  • Direct input into what MOVR 2.0 becomes

For MDA:

  • Data-driven vendor selection (not assumptions)
  • Community-centered design approach
  • Foundation for accelerated research partnerships

For Research:

  • Engaged cohort ready to contribute data
  • Platform infrastructure for future studies
  • Modernized data collection capabilities

How It Works

Family Signs Up → Auto-Assigned to Vendor → Creates Account → Provides Feedback
                         │
                   Fair quota system ensures
                   all 3 vendors get evaluated

Vendor Partners:

  1. Vibrent Health - Priority platform
  2. Citizen Health - Secondary evaluation
  3. Unite Genomics - Tertiary evaluation

Each vendor receives minimum guaranteed participants before priority order applies, ensuring fair comparison.
