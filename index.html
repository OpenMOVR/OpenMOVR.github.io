The MOVR Journey

From pioneering registry to next-generation research platform

🏛️ What Was MOVR?

MOVR was a groundbreaking neuromuscular disease registry that collected real-world clinical data across seven conditions, building the largest longitudinal dataset of its kind.

MOVR 2.0 is the next evolution - moving from manual data entry to automated EMR integration, from closed systems to open source tools, from institutional to community-driven development.

OpenMOVR's Role

Where open science meets neuromuscular disease research

🔗 Bridging Communities

OpenMOVR serves as the open source extension of MDA's MOVR registry, connecting researchers, developers, and innovators who want to contribute to neuromuscular disease research.

We provide the collaborative infrastructure for building next-generation research tools - from FHIR integration libraries to AI-powered analytics - while maintaining the trust and data governance that patients expect.

🔬 Where We're Heading: Advanced Applications

Post-Market Intelligence & Pharmacovigilance

Challenge: 58% of hospitalizations marked 'other' - unclear safety signals

  • FDA-Reportable Adverse Event Classifications: Custom ontology transforming 58% 'other' classifications into regulatory-compliant categories
  • Enhanced Spinraza Safety Detection: Automated detection of procedure-related vs. disease progression events
  • Head-to-Head Comparisons: Real-world effectiveness across Spinraza, Zolgensma, and Evrysdi with complete adverse event histories
Clinical Development & Patient Journeys

Challenge: Incomplete gross motor milestones (35-50% missing data)

  • Enhanced Milestone Tracking: Identify ambulatory Type 2/3 SMA patients with sufficient developmental histories from EMR notes
Enhanced Biomarker Discovery

Challenge: Missing SMN2 copy data (47% unknown in legacy MOVR)

  • EMR-Derived Genetic Correlation: Cross-reference SMN2 copy number with motor milestone progression across 300+ SMA patients
  • Advanced Phenotyping: Correlate MRI muscle fat fraction changes with CHOP-INTEND scores using longitudinal imaging

MOVR's Mission as a Trusted Data Steward

Using the power of MDA's brand, its Care Centers and technology partners, act as a TRUSTED DATA STEWARD of valuable health data by offering a comprehensive solution for data collection, transformation and harmonization which produces valuable fit-for-purpose datasets to be used to enhance clinical trial efficiency, streamline drug discovery and approval processes.
