MOVR Update: MOVR 2.0 is Coming!

Important Update

üîÑ We're evolving!

After over a decade of collecting critical data on neuromuscular diseases, the original MOVR Data Hub has officially closed. This marks an exciting transition to the next phase: MOVR 2.0: A new generation platform.

What You Need to Know:

  • ‚úÖ The original MOVR Data Hub is now closed.
  • ‚úÖ Participant enrollment and data collection through the original study has ended.
  • üöÄ We are launching MOVR 2.0 ‚Äî a new generation platform that is more advanced, tech-forward, and sustainable.
  • üî¨ We're currently running operational validation pilots leading to a Q2 2026 go-live decision.

Were you part of MOVR?

If you were previously enrolled, your participation has concluded, and your consent has been withdrawn. But we'd love for you to join us in the next chapter!

üß™ Current Status: Validation Phase

We're not rushing into full deployment. Instead, we're running a small precursor pilot program to operationally validate the safest and most effective approach. Technology is rapidly evolving, and we're at the right place at the right time to explore new processes that will have great impact.

üéØ We Are Enrolling!

Yes, we have active, but capped enrollment in our validation pilot. This helps us test our new systems while contributing to real research.

üí° Why Careful Approach?

Registries are expensive and critical to research. We need to ensure sustainability and effectiveness before full-scale launch.

üåê Introducing OpenMOVR

As part of MOVR 2.0, we're launching a new governance framework that will democratize registry research and promote collaboration across the neuromuscular disease community.

ü§ù Community-Driven Innovation

OpenMOVR is our commitment to avoiding silos and embracing collaboration. Like any good registry, there is governance‚Äîbut entering new technology frontiers requires more complex solutions, and we're democratizing that with our new approach.

What OpenMOVR Will Provide:

  • Frequent updates: Regular posts on publications and research progress
  • SDK toolkits: Developer tools for the research community (when ready)
  • Collaboration opportunities: Ways for community members to get involved
  • Cross-PAG collaboration: Working with other patient advocacy groups
  • Community expert input: Leaning on experts who care about the mission

Governance Model: MDA will always have final say on decisions, but we'll gather thoughts and input from our community‚Äîa model that has been done effectively and we want to put into practice, scaling feasibly and practically.
