Platform Vision: The Future of Registry Research

Technical Vision

üöÄ Next Generation Registry Platform

Moving from manual data entry to automated EMR integration while maintaining gold standard data quality and building open source tools for the entire neuromuscular disease research community.

Vision Overview

The next generation MOVR platform represents a fundamental shift in how registry data is collected, processed, and accessed:

üè• From Manual to Automated

Current Challenges

  • Time-intensive: Manual data entry limits site participation
  • Error-prone: Duplicate entry increases inconsistencies
  • Limited scale: Manual processes don't scale with research needs
  • Site burden: Additional workload affects participation

Automated Solution

  • EMR Integration: Direct data pull from electronic medical records
  • FHIR Standards: Standardized healthcare data interoperability
  • Real-time Processing: Continuous data capture and validation
  • Reduced Burden: Minimal impact on clinical workflows

üåç From Siloed to Collaborative

Current Limitations

  • Access barriers: Complex approval processes
  • Limited tools: Restricted analysis capabilities
  • Siloed research: Isolated studies and findings
  • Closed development: Limited community innovation

Open Collaboration

  • Open Source Tools: Community-developed research platform
  • APIs & SDKs: Developer-friendly access and integration
  • Standardized Data: Interoperable across studies and platforms
  • Transparent Development: Community-driven innovation
